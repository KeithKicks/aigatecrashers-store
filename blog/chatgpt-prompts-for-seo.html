ChatGPT Prompts for SEO: 10 Prompts to Rank Higher in 2026

AI Gatecrashers Team · April 20, 2026

SEO is a grind. Keyword research, content optimization, meta tag writing, internal linking strategy, technical audits — the workload is relentless, especially for small teams and solo operators. ChatGPT doesn't replace SEO tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush, but it dramatically accelerates the thinking and writing work that sits between data analysis and published content.

This guide gives you 10 specific ChatGPT prompts for the most impactful SEO tasks, explains why each one works, and shows you how to integrate them into your SEO workflow. These aren't vague suggestions — they're copy-paste prompts you can use today.

How ChatGPT Fits Into an SEO Workflow

Before the prompts, a quick framework for where AI helps and where it doesn't in SEO.

Where ChatGPT excels: Content ideation, keyword clustering, content briefs, meta tag writing, content optimization, FAQ generation, schema markup creation, internal linking suggestions, and content gap analysis. These are all tasks that require thinking and writing — ChatGPT's sweet spot.

Where you still need dedicated tools: Actual search volume data, backlink analysis, rank tracking, site crawl audits, and competitor keyword intelligence. ChatGPT doesn't have access to real-time search data, so use it alongside your SEO tools, not instead of them.

The best SEO workflow in 2026 uses dedicated tools for data and ChatGPT for strategy and execution. Data tells you what to target. ChatGPT helps you execute faster and better.

Prompt 1: Keyword Clustering and Content Planning

"I have a list of keywords related to [topic/niche]. Group them into topical clusters based on search intent. For each cluster, suggest a pillar page topic and 3-5 supporting content topics. Indicate the likely search intent for each (informational, commercial, transactional, navigational). Here are the keywords: [paste your keyword list]."

Keyword clustering is one of the most time-consuming parts of SEO planning. This prompt does in minutes what used to take a spreadsheet and an afternoon. The search intent classification is especially valuable because it determines the content type — informational keywords need blog posts, commercial keywords need comparison pages, transactional keywords need product pages.

Prompt 2: SEO Content Brief

"Create a detailed SEO content brief for a blog post targeting the keyword '[target keyword].' Include: a recommended title tag (under 60 characters), a meta description (under 155 characters), the recommended H1, a content outline with H2 and H3 subheadings, the target word count, 5 semantically related keywords to include naturally, 3 internal linking opportunities, and 2 external authority sources to reference. The target audience is [describe audience]."

Content briefs are the bridge between SEO strategy and content creation. This prompt generates a comprehensive brief that any writer — human or AI — can follow to produce a page that's structured for rankings from the start. The semantic keyword suggestions help with topical relevance, which is increasingly important for Google's understanding of content depth.

Prompt 3: Title Tag and Meta Description Generator

"Write 5 title tag variations and 3 meta description variations for a page about [topic] targeting the keyword '[keyword].' Title tags must be under 60 characters and include the primary keyword near the beginning. Meta descriptions must be under 155 characters and include a compelling reason to click. Avoid clickbait. Make them accurate and specific."

Title tags and meta descriptions directly impact click-through rates from search results. Most people write one version and move on. This prompt gives you multiple options to test, and the constraints ensure they're optimized for SERP display. The "avoid clickbait" instruction prevents ChatGPT from writing sensationalized tags that might get clicks but increase bounce rates.

Prompt 4: Content Optimization Audit

"Analyze the following blog post for SEO optimization. Check for: keyword usage and placement (primary keyword: '[keyword]'), heading structure (H1, H2, H3 hierarchy), content depth and comprehensiveness, readability and paragraph length, internal linking opportunities, and suggestions for adding FAQ schema content. Here's the content: [paste your content]."

This is an on-page audit prompt. Paste in an existing piece of content and ChatGPT will evaluate it against SEO best practices. It won't check technical factors like page speed or mobile-friendliness — that's what Google Search Console and PageSpeed Insights are for. But for content-level optimization, it catches things human editors miss: keyword cannibalization between headings, thin sections that need expansion, and missing semantic terms.

Prompt 5: FAQ Section Generator

"Generate 8 frequently asked questions about [topic] that people are likely searching for on Google. For each question, write a concise, authoritative answer in 2-3 sentences. Format the output as HTML using FAQ schema-compatible structure. The answers should be factual, specific, and directly address the question without filler."

FAQ sections serve two purposes: they capture long-tail question keywords and they're eligible for rich results in Google. The schema markup request means ChatGPT outputs the HTML structure you can implement directly, saving the step of manually wrapping the content in schema tags. Prompt 6: Internal Linking Strategy

"I have a website about [niche] with the following pages: [list your main pages/posts with their topics]. Suggest an internal linking strategy. For each page, recommend 2-3 other pages it should link to, the anchor text to use, and where in the content the link should be placed (introduction, body, conclusion). Prioritize links that help users navigate from awareness-stage content to commercial-stage content."

Internal linking is one of the most underused SEO tactics. It distributes page authority, helps Google understand your site structure, and guides users toward conversion pages. This prompt generates a complete internal linking plan. The instruction to "prioritize links from awareness to commercial content" ensures your link structure supports the buying journey, not just SEO metrics.

Prompt 7: Competitor Content Gap Analysis

"I'm competing with [competitor URL or description] in the [niche] space. Based on the common topics covered by sites in this niche, suggest 10 content topics that would fill gaps in a typical [niche] blog. For each topic, provide: a suggested title, the target keyword, the likely search intent, and a one-sentence content angle that differentiates from typical coverage. Focus on topics with commercial intent."

Content gap analysis identifies topics your competitors cover that you don't — and topics nobody covers well. While ChatGPT can't check actual rankings, it understands niche topical coverage extremely well. The "differentiate from typical coverage" instruction pushes it to suggest unique angles rather than carbon copies of what already exists.

Prompt 8: Schema Markup Generator

"Generate JSON-LD schema markup for a [type: Article/Product/FAQ/HowTo/LocalBusiness] page. Details: [provide the relevant information — title, author, date, description, steps, etc.]. Output clean, valid JSON-LD that can be pasted directly into the page's head section. Include all recommended properties for this schema type."

Schema markup helps Google understand your content and enables rich results. Most small site owners skip it because writing JSON-LD by hand is tedious and error-prone. This prompt generates valid, complete schema markup in seconds. Especially useful for Article, FAQ, HowTo, and Product schemas — the types most likely to trigger rich results.

Prompt 9: Blog Post Outline for Featured Snippets

"Create a blog post outline optimized to win the featured snippet for the query '[target query].' The current featured snippet type for this kind of query is likely [paragraph/list/table]. Structure the outline so that: the H1 includes or closely matches the query, there's a concise 40-60 word paragraph directly answering the query near the top of the content (for paragraph snippets), or a clear numbered/bulleted list (for list snippets). Then include additional H2 sections that expand on the topic with depth and related subtopics."

Featured snippets are position zero — they appear above all organic results. Winning them requires specific content formatting. This prompt structures your content to match the snippet format Google expects for your target query. The concise answer near the top gives Google the extractable text it needs, while the expanded sections provide the depth that supports overall rankings.

Prompt 10: Link Building Outreach Email

"Write 3 variations of a link building outreach email for [your content/resource]. The email should: open with a genuine, specific compliment about the recipient's site (leave a placeholder for me to customize), explain what my content is and why it's relevant to their audience, make a specific and reasonable ask (link insertion, resource page addition, or guest post), and keep the total length under 150 words. Tone: professional but personal, not templated. No fake urgency or manipulation."

Link building outreach is one of the most tedious parts of SEO. This prompt generates natural-sounding outreach emails that avoid the spammy templates most people send. The placeholder for a specific compliment forces you to personalize each email — the single biggest factor in outreach response rates. Three variations give you options to test which approach gets more replies.

Building Your SEO Prompt System

Individual prompts are useful. A systematic prompt library is transformative. Here's how to build one:

Organize by SEO phase. Group your prompts into categories: keyword research, content planning, content creation, on-page optimization, technical SEO, and link building. This mirrors your actual workflow and makes it easy to find the right prompt at the right time.

Save winning variations. When a prompt produces particularly good output, save the exact version with notes on what made it work. Over time, your prompt library becomes a highly refined SEO playbook.

Combine with data tools. The most powerful SEO workflow: pull data from Ahrefs/SEMrush, feed it into ChatGPT prompts, and use the AI-generated strategy and content to execute. Data informs strategy; AI accelerates execution.
